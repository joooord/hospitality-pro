Martyn's Law Quick Reference

The Act creates two tiers based on venue capacity. Know your tier — the obligations differ significantly.

Standard Tier

Venues with capacity 200–799 people. Must implement basic security measures and train staff in Counter-Terrorism Awareness.

Enhanced Tier

Venues with capacity 800+ people. Full documented risk assessment, security plan, and appointed Security Manager required.

Key contacts: Your local Counter Terrorism Security Advisor (CTSA) — free, confidential advice from police. ACT Awareness e-learning via gov.uk/protect. ProtectUK platform for training resources.

Counter-Terrorism Risk Assessment

Work through each section systematically. Your answers are saved automatically and can be exported as a formal risk assessment document.

1
Venue Overview & Crowd Profile

Establish the baseline — who visits, when, and in what numbers. This defines your threat profile.

2
Entry & Exit Points

Document all ways people can enter or exit the venue. Include service entrances, fire exits, and any access routes that may be exploited.

3
Vulnerable Areas & Crowding Points

Identify areas that could be targeted or become dangerous in an emergency. Think about choke points, queuing areas, and places where crowds gather.

4
Current Security Measures

Document what is already in place. Honest assessment — gaps identified here become your action plan.

5
Threat & Risk Assessment

Based on your venue type, location, and profile — what are the most likely threats? Rate likelihood and impact honestly.

Emergency Procedures

Venue-specific action cards for each threat scenario. Customise with your venue layout, contact details, and specific protocols. These can be printed and posted in staff areas.
